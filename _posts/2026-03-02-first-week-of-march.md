Finally got onto the allotment after what felt like weeks of rain. The ground has dried out enough to work without compacting it too badly. Spent most of Saturday turning the three main beds over and working in a barrow-load of well-rotted compost from the far bin.

**Jobs done this week:**

- Turned compost into beds 1, 2 and 3
- Raked bed 4 level and covered with cardboard to suppress early weeds
- Oiled and sharpened the fork and spade — long overdue
- Placed final seed order: Gardeners Delight tomatoes, Cobra French bean, Tender and True parsnip

In the greenhouse, the leeks I sowed back in January are looking decent — about 8cm tall now. I'll start hardening them off in April. The onion seeds I sowed in February are just starting to show above the compost. Slow but steady.

Next weekend: get the first early potatoes in if the soil temperature is above 7°C.
