A solid day on the plot and in the greenhouse. The priority today was getting the tomatoes started — I've been holding off because last year's seedlings got too leggy on the windowsill before I had space to pot them on properly.

**Jobs done this week:**

- Sowed 12 x Gardeners Delight tomato seeds in propagator at 20°C
- Sowed 6 x Marconi Rosso pepper seeds (slow germinators — fingers crossed)
- Sowed a tray of basil tucked at the back of the propagator for warmth
- Washed down the greenhouse staging with diluted Jeyes fluid
- Re-organised shelf layout to give propagator a permanent warm spot

Outside, the broad beans I sowed in modules back in February have been moved to the cold frame to harden off. They're looking stocky and healthy — no signs of the blackfly that plagued them last summer.

The rhubarb crown in the far corner is just starting to push through — always a welcome sign. I'll leave it one more year before harvesting.

**Jobs for next week:** chit the first early potatoes, direct sow parsnips, clear the overwintered spinach bed.
